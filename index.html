Hallo! I’m Emily Sartorius

I am a student at the University of Michigan School of Information studying Library and Information Science. When I grow up I want to work in an academic library teaching students how to research effectively!


I have my Bachelor of Arts in German, and so I was interested in pursuing an internship in a German-speaking country. I was the intern at the Humboldt-Elsevier Advanced Data & Text Centre (HEADT Centre) in Berlin. Through my time at the HEADT Centre, I learned about the intricacies of international publishing and the planning needed to create an effective database project. I learned how to plan an experiment, effectively communicate across a team, collaborate writing styles, and respond to peer-review comments. I also experienced using metadata standards and working within a Beta Database. I would like to thank the School of Information and the Center for European Studies at the University of Michigan for their financial support. Click through my site if you would like to learn more about this international experience!
